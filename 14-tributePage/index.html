Ahilya Bai Holkar's life:

  • Ahilyabai Holkar was born on 31 May 1725 in the village of Chaundi, in the present-day Ahmednagar district in Maharashtra.
  • She was married to Khanderao Holkar in 1735.
  • In 1745, she gave birth to their son Malerao. Malerao was mentally unwell and died of his illness in 1767.
  • Her husband was killed during the siege of Kumher in 1754.
  • After his death in 1754, his father Malhar Rao prevented his wife Ahilya Bai from committing sati.
  • Malhar Rao Holkar died in 1766, 12 years after the death of his son Khanderao.
  • Malhar Rao's grandson and Khanderao's only son Malerao Holkar became the ruler of Indore in 1766, under the regentship of Ahilyabai, but he too died within few months on 5 April 1767.
  • Ahilyabai became the ruler of Indore after the death of her son Khanderao.
  • Some in Malwa objected to her assumption of rule, but the army of Holkar supported her leadership.
  • Among Ahilyabai's accomplishments was the development of Indore from a small village to a prosperous and beautiful city; her own capital, however, was in nearby Maheshwar, a town on the banks of the Narmada river.
  • She also built forts and roads in Malwa, sponsored festivals and gave donations for regular worship in many Hindu temples.
  • Outside Malwa, she built dozens of temples, ghats, wells, tanks and rest-houses across an area stretching from the Himalayas to pilgrimage centres in South India.
  • Ahilyabai died on 13 August 1795 at the age of 70. A woman of modern times, Ahilyabai's rule is remembered as a golden age in Indore's and India's history.
